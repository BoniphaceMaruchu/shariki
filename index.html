What Shariki is

A practical bridge between early traction and growth readiness.

Shariki is not trying to be everything in the entrepreneurship ecosystem. It is a focused venture-building MVP for a small number of serious entrepreneurs who need the execution layer that helps real businesses become stronger.

Shariki is
  • Hands-on business-building support for selected entrepreneurs with traction
  • A practical execution partner for founders working through real bottlenecks
  • A small MVP designed to learn deeply before scaling
  • A bridge between early traction and growth readiness
Shariki is not
  • Not a generic training program
  • Not a grant fund
  • Not a startup competition
  • Not a broad incubator accepting everyone
What discovery revealed

The missing middle is execution.

Entrepreneur discovery showed that the main gap is not lack of ideas. Many local founders are already active, capable, and serving real customers. The harder gap is turning early traction into disciplined, durable growth.

The biggest gap is not lack of ideas. It is the execution layer between early traction and durable growth.

The gap is not ambition. The gap is the operating support around serious founders.

Shariki is designed to provide practical proximity, accountability, and trusted connections for entrepreneurs serving underserved communities.

Execution capacity

Founders are active and committed, but many lack repeatable systems, clear priorities, and disciplined follow-through.

Financial readiness

Many businesses need stronger tracking, costing, reporting, and decision-making before they can responsibly pursue future growth.

Market access

Founders often need practical support to reach customers, buyers, suppliers, institutions, and distribution channels.

Team and operations

Several businesses are constrained by team capacity, role clarity, quality control, and operational consistency.

Partnership readiness

Many founders need help becoming credible enough for funders, buyers, mentors, and strategic partners.

Reporting readiness

Shariki helps founders build the evidence, records, and basic compliance habits that make future support easier to justify.

Who Shariki is looking for

Serious entrepreneurs with real customers and a clear growth bottleneck.

The first MVP should prioritize founders who are already doing the hard work of building, selling, learning, and serving people. Shariki adds discipline, support, and connections where they can make the biggest difference.

  • Have an existing business, product, or service already serving real customers
  • Are building something that can expand access for underserved communities
  • Have a clear growth bottleneck that hands-on support can help solve
  • Are coachable, committed, and willing to meet regularly
  • Can share basic business data and track progress
  • Show potential to create jobs, improve affordability, strengthen local supply chains, or reach people currently left out
The first Shariki MVP

Start in Arusha. Support a few founders well. Learn before scaling.

Shariki should be understood as early and focused. The first pilot is meant to test whether close, practical support can help promising businesses become more disciplined, more resilient, and more ready for future growth.

A small 90-day venture-building pilot

Shariki will begin with a small 90-day venture-building pilot in Arusha, supporting 3-5 selected entrepreneurs. The goal is to build proof, learning, systems, and founder outcomes before expanding.

How the support works

A practical cadence for building stronger businesses.

Shariki's work is direct, consistent, and evidence-oriented. The pilot focuses on what founders can improve week by week: numbers, customers, priorities, operating habits, and the right relationships.

01

Diagnose

Understand the business, bottlenecks, numbers, customers, founder priorities, and growth constraints.

02

Build

Support weekly execution through priorities, scorecards, financial discipline, customer learning, and operating systems.

03

Connect

Open doors to mentors, partners, suppliers, customers, and ecosystem actors who can remove real constraints to growth.

04

Document

Track progress, founder learning, business improvements, and evidence for future partners.

What success looks like

Progress should show up in founder discipline and community access.

Shariki should not measure success by activity alone. The early proof should come from stronger founder behavior, clearer business systems, and practical signs that the model can improve access for people who are often left out.
