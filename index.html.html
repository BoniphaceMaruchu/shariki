The problem

Too many promising entrepreneurs stay too small for too long.

Across Tanzania and East Africa, capable founders are already trying to make essential goods and services easier to reach. But many never get the combination of practical mentorship, operating discipline, market access, trusted networks, and early catalytic support needed to truly grow.

◎

What gets lost today

The current support landscape often leans toward broad entrepreneurship awareness, generic training, or traditional small-business support. Those efforts matter, but they rarely build the kind of market-creating companies that can lower costs, reach non-consumers, create jobs, and shift access at meaningful scale.

“The gap is not only talent. The gap is the system around promising founders — the discipline, support, and connections that help a real business become a scalable one.”
Shariki’s response is to build that system with focus, proximity, and execution.
Why Shariki

A narrower, more catalytic model.

Shariki is designed for a smaller number of high-potential founders whose businesses can expand access for people currently priced out, underserved, or left beyond the reach of existing solutions.

↗

Active discovery

Shariki will not rely only on passive applications. It looks across universities, technical institutions, local business communities, referrals, hubs, and partner networks to find entrepreneurs already doing something meaningfully different in the market.

◌

Market-creating lens

The focus is not on copying familiar businesses. It is on ventures that can make essential products and services cheaper, simpler, and more reachable for underserved communities.

✓

Execution discipline

Founders receive practical venture-building support: coaching, operating systems, customer learning, financial tracking, accountability, and the partnerships needed to remove growth bottlenecks.

How it works

Shariki is built to help real businesses become stronger companies.

The model combines founder selection, hands-on venture support, mentor and partner connections, and a pathway toward future investment readiness. It is intentionally practical and grounded in what early-stage founders actually need to keep moving.

1

Find the right founders

Shariki actively searches for entrepreneurs already serving real customers and showing signs that their model could unlock broader access in the market.

2

Build with them

Each founder receives practical support around venture diagnostics, customer insight, execution systems, financial discipline, and steady accountability.

3

Open the right doors

Shariki connects founders to mentors, suppliers, strategic partners, and ecosystem actors who can help remove real constraints to growth.

4

Prepare for what comes next

Over time, the platform is designed to support investment readiness and aligned financing pathways where they fit the entrepreneur and the market.

Pilot vision

Start small. Learn deeply. Build something repeatable.

The first Shariki pilot is planned for Arusha in 2026 with approximately 5–10 founders. The goal is not to move fast for appearances. The goal is to build a model with enough quality, discipline, and proof to earn long-term trust.

⌂

What the first pilot is meant to test

  • Founder discovery and selection criteria
  • Coaching cadence and venture diagnostics
  • Mentor matching and partner engagement
  • Simple measurement systems that track practical outcomes
⟲

Why the pilot matters

Shariki is being built with the ambition to last. That means the first cohort is as much about learning, refining, and building trust as it is about supporting founders. The early design choices should make future growth stronger, not noisier.

2026

Launch pilot in Arusha

What success looks like

Shariki measures progress through real founder and community outcomes.

The ambition is not to produce activity for its own sake. It is to support ventures that become more disciplined, more resilient, and more capable of expanding access in the lives of everyday people.
